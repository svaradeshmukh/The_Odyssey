This paper aims to use word embedding models, word frequency, and correlation features to evaluate the ethical landscape of The Odyssey, focusing on justice and retribution. The Odyssey is an ancient Greek epic poem typically attributed to the poet Homer but is the result of centuries of oral storytelling tradition. While there has been extensive study on retribution in The Odyssey as well as how ideas of fate and destiny are represented in Homeric epic, the intersection of these two major themes has been understudied in relation to one another, particularly through a computational lens. This study introduces literary text mining methods as a means to formulate literary arguments grounded in quantitative evidence. Our results showed a strong correlation between ideas of fate and retribution, suggesting that vengeance in The Odyssey is carefully planned and morally permissible rather than an emotional reaction to wrongdoing. We hope our results will encourage future projects that use computational methods to provide new perspectives on literary questions in Homeric literature.
